Privacy policy for PocketLink

Last updated: September 16, 2025

This privacy policy explains how PocketLink ("we," "us," or "our") handles your information when you use our browser extension. Your privacy and trust are very important to us.

What information we collect

PocketLink is designed to be as private as possible. The only piece of data we store is your Bit.ly Generic Access Token.

  • Bit.ly access token: This token is required to create shortlinks on your behalf. You provide this token voluntarily on the extension's options page.

We do **not** collect, store, or transmit any other personal information, browsing history, or page content.

How we use your information

  • Your **Bit.ly access token** is used exclusively to make authenticated API calls to the Bit.ly service (`https://api-ssl.bitly.com/`) for the sole purpose of shortening URLs as you request.

How we store your information

Your Bit.ly access token is stored locally on your computer using the sandboxed chrome.storage.sync API provided by your browser. This method has two key benefits:

  1. Security: The data is stored securely by your browser and is not accessible by other websites or extensions.
  2. Convenience: If you are signed into your browser's account (e.g., your Google Account), this token will sync securely across your devices, so you only need to set it up once.

We never see or have access to this token. It is stored and used entirely within your own browser.

Third-party services

The PocketLink extension communicates with one third-party service: the Bit.ly API. This is necessary for the core functionality of the extension. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of Bit.ly. You can review Bit.ly's privacy policy here.

Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
